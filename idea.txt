Your idea is basically a time-based rolling ATM short straddle.
Conceptually, you are trying to:

Always stay near the highest theta decay zone (ATM)
Keep the position “delta neutral”
Continuously adapt to market movement

That’s a valid professional-style options framework. Many market makers and systematic option sellers use variations of this.

But the details decide whether this becomes:

a stable theta harvesting system, or
a commission/slippage machine that gets destroyed on trending days.
What Your Strategy Is Doing

At 9:30:

Sell ATM CE + ATM PE

Every 15 minutes:

Check whether ATM strike changed
If yes:
Exit old straddle
Sell new ATM straddle

Example:

NIFTY at 24,000 → Sell 24,000 CE + PE
After 15 mins NIFTY moves to 24,120
ATM becomes 24,100 or 24,150
Exit 24,000 straddle
Sell new ATM straddle

So you are continuously “re-centering” around spot.

The Good Parts
1. Keeps Delta Neutral

This is the biggest strength.

A static short straddle becomes directional very quickly when market trends.

Your approach tries to reduce that drift.

2. Captures Fresh Theta

ATM options decay fastest.

Rolling back to ATM repeatedly keeps you in the best theta zone.

3. Avoids Deep ITM/OTM Problems

Without rolling:

One side becomes dangerous ITM
Other side becomes worthless

Rolling prevents carrying ugly skewed risk.

The Major Problems

This is where most rolling systems fail.

1. Whipsaw / Overtrading Problem

This is the biggest flaw.

Suppose market does this:

24,000
24,080
24,010
24,090
24,020

Your system keeps:

exiting
re-entering
paying slippage
paying brokerage
paying bid-ask spread

You can lose money even when market ultimately goes nowhere.

This is extremely common in intraday index markets.

2. You Are Selling Volatility During Expansion

When market trends strongly:

implied volatility expands
gamma risk explodes

Your strategy keeps selling fresh ATM premium into momentum.

This can become dangerous on:

RBI days
Budget
Fed events
expiry trend days
3. Gap Risk Between Checks

You are checking every 15 mins.

What if:

market moves 150 points in 3 mins?

You’ll hold a losing straddle until next check.

This is a hidden risk.

4. Transaction Cost Explosion

Rolling frequently means:

brokerage
STT
exchange fees
slippage
spread crossing

This silently kills many intraday systems.

Especially in India.

5. Gamma Risk Near Expiry

Near expiry:

ATM premiums move violently
delta changes rapidly

Rolling ATM continuously on expiry can become extremely unstable.

How to Make This Much Better

This is where strategy design matters.

Improvement 1 — Use Price Movement Threshold Instead of Time

Instead of:

“Check every 15 mins”

Do:

“Roll only if underlying moves X points”

Example:

Roll only if NIFTY moves 0.4% from last entry
Or 80 points
Or 1 ATR unit

This reduces useless churn.

This is a much more professional approach.

Improvement 2 — Add Trend Filter

This is VERY important.

Avoid short straddles during strong trends.

Example filters:

VWAP trend
EMA slope
ADX
Opening range breakout
India VIX expansion

Example:

If ADX > 25 → stop rolling straddles
If price far from VWAP → avoid entry

This alone can massively improve survivability.

Improvement 3 — Don’t Roll Entire Position

Instead of:

exit both legs

Try:

roll only challenged side

Example:

Market moves up strongly
Roll CE side upward
Keep PE

This reduces:

transaction cost
overtrading
theta reset losses

Many professional desks do this.

Improvement 4 — Use Delta-Based Rolling

This is more advanced and much smarter.

Instead of:

“ATM changed”

Use:

“Roll when position delta exceeds threshold”

Example:

If net delta exceeds ±10
Rebalance

This is closer to institutional gamma scalping logic.

Improvement 5 — Add Volatility Regime Logic

Your strategy works better in:

range-bound
moderate IV crush
low trend environments

Avoid:

event days
trending expiry days
gap-heavy sessions

Possible rule:

Only run when India VIX below threshold
Avoid first 30 mins on high gap days
Improvement 6 — Use Hedged Straddles

Pure naked short straddles are dangerous.

Instead:

Short ATM straddle
Buy far OTM wings

Turns it into:

Iron fly / iron condor style

You reduce tail risk dramatically.

Improvement 7 — Add Daily Loss Lock

Mandatory.

Example:

Stop strategy after:
1.5x average daily profit loss
or 2 consecutive failed rolls

Otherwise one trend day can wipe weeks of theta gains.

Very Important Hidden Issue

Every roll resets theta.

Example:

You sell premium
Time decay starts helping
Then you exit early and re-enter fresh premium

You may unintentionally:

keep buying back partially decayed options
and selling fresh expensive gamma repeatedly

This is subtle but critical.

A Better Version of Your Strategy

A more robust version might be:

Entry
9:30 AM
Sell ATM straddle
Roll Condition

Roll ONLY IF:

Spot moves > 0.5%
OR
Net delta exceeds threshold
Trend Filter

No new rolls if:

ADX high
far from VWAP
strong candle expansion
Risk
Hedge wings mandatory
Daily MTM stop
Max number of rolls per day
Exit
3:00 PM hard exit
Where This Strategy Usually Works Best

Best conditions:

sideways market
slow grinding market
IV crush days
post-event calm sessions

Worst conditions:

trend days
breakout days
expiry momentum
macro news days
Final Assessment

The core idea is good.
It’s actually a legitimate systematic options concept.

But:

    a pure “roll every time ATM changes” system will probably overtrade badly.
    the biggest enemies are:
    gamma
slippage
trend days
excessive rolling

The most important upgrade is:

Replace time-based rolling with movement/delta-based rolling + trend filters.

That changes it from a retail idea into something closer to professional volatility trading logic.
